# Agile-Project
A repository of the Sprint Review and Retrospective and agile representation for the ChadaTech corporation in an effort to justify adopting the Scrum framework over the less successful waterfall methodology.
# Q/A Portfolio
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
---
For me, I identified keywords in the users' feedback and used the decomposition problem-solving strategy to gain a full understanding of their needs.
Creating the user stories helped with this by allowing me to methodically organize their needs into separate cases, each with their own assigned priority values and estimated effort needed to complete them. 
This allows me to prioritize developing and delivering functionality closely aligned to their needs starting on the first iteration and allow me to quickly adapt to changing priorities.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
---
I approach developing programs by identifying keywords, utilizing decomposition to turn complex problems into simpler sub-problems, and getting clarifying feedback on any implementation details.
This has helped me fully understand systems and how they could be implemented from a developer mindset as well as overcome mental blocks.
Some of the agile processes that I hope to utilize in my future development work is creating and refining user stories and test cases based on user stories. 
This is because I found out they are useful in determining priorities, adapting to changes, and ensuring I create rebust and secure code.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
---
This is a great question. 
I think if I were describe beign a good team member is, it doesn't just involve clear and concise communication, the ability to quickly adapt, and designing, implementing, and testing secure and rebust code.
It also involves actively dismantling internalized biases and prejudices, identifying and dismantling hierarchical power structures, centering worker autonomy, and creating ethical software.
All of these things don't make a good team member, they make a great team member.
Otherwise, we don't make a real difference in our and other's lives.
