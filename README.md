# Agile-Project
A repository of the Sprint Review and Retrospective and agile representation for the ChadaTech corporation in an effort to justify adopting the Scrum framework over the less successful waterfall methodology.
# Q/A Portfolio
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
---
I identify keywords in user feedback, decompose problems into smaller parts, and iterate on clarifying questions.
Creating the user stories helped with this by allowing me to methodically organize their needs into separate cases, each with their own assigned priority and effort estimates. 
This allows me to prioritize developing and delivering functionality closely aligned to their needs early and quickly adapt to changing priorities.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
---
I break complex problems into sub-problems, seek clarifying feedback, and use iterative development.
This has helped me fully understand systems and how they could be implemented from a developer mindset as well as overcome mental blocks.
I plan to create and refine user stories, product backlogs, and test cases because they are useful in determining priorities, adapting to changes, and creating robust and secure code.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
---
A good team member uses clear and concise communication, the ability to quickly adapt, and creates secure, reliable code.
A great team member actively dismantles internalized biases and prejudices, identifies and dismantles hierarchical power structures, centers worker autonomy, and creates ethical software, not just profit.
Otherwise, we don't make a real difference in our lives and the work we do.
